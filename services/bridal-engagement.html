Choosing Your Diamond
The 4 Cs — Simply Explained

Understanding diamond quality doesn't need to be complicated. Here's what actually matters when choosing your center stone.

C
Cut
The most important factor. A well-cut diamond reflects light beautifully. Poor cutting makes even a large diamond look dull.
C
Color
Graded D (colorless) to Z (light yellow). For most buyers, G-H range offers near-colorless appearance at significantly better value.
C
Clarity
Internal characteristics called inclusions. VS2 to SI1 are typically eye-clean — no visible flaws — at a more accessible price than Flawless grades.
C
Carat
Carat is the diamond's weight, not its size. A well-cut 0.90ct can look larger than a poorly-cut 1.0ct. We'll show you the difference in person.

Lab-Grown Diamonds —
More Ring for Less

Lab-grown diamonds have the same chemical composition, hardness, and optical brilliance as mined diamonds. The only difference is origin — and price. A lab-grown 1.5ct diamond can cost 60-70% less than its mined equivalent. That means a bigger stone, a more elaborate setting, or both. We offer both types and will help you understand your options without pressure.

Common Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
How much should I spend on an engagement ring?
Spend what makes sense for your financial situation — not what a marketing rule tells you. The "two months' salary" rule is an old De Beers marketing campaign, not a meaningful guideline. We work with all budgets and will help you get the best possible ring for what you're comfortable spending.
How do I know what size ring to get?
There are several tricks for getting her ring size without spoiling the surprise — borrowing a ring she wears on her ring finger, asking a close friend or family member, or coming in and we'll help you estimate. Rings can always be resized after the proposal if needed.
Should I propose first or let her help design the ring?
Both approaches have merit. If she has very specific taste, involving her in the design often leads to a ring she's happiest with. Many couples come in together after the proposal to design the ring as a shared experience. We accommodate both styles.
What are the most popular engagement ring styles?
Currently popular styles include: solitaire (timeless, minimalist), halo (diamond surrounded by smaller diamonds for a larger look), vintage/Art Deco (milgrain, filigree), three-stone (representing past, present, future), and East-West oval settings. We'll show you examples and discuss what suits her personality.
Do you offer financing or payment plans?
Please call us or stop in to discuss options. We understand that engagement rings are a significant purchase and we're committed to finding a way to make your vision achievable.
