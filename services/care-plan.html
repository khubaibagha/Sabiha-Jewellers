Why Regular Care Matters
Keep Your Jewelry
Safe & Beautiful

Your fine jewelry works hard every day. Rings take constant impact and friction; necklaces tug and flex; earrings and bracelets experience wear over time. What looks perfect today may have a loosening prong, a thinning shank, or a developing crack that isn't visible to the naked eye.

The JM Care Plan is our commitment to keeping your jewelry in peak condition year-round. Regular professional cleanings remove built-up oils, lotions, and residue that dull your stones. Routine inspections catch developing issues before you lose a stone or need a major repair.

It's the difference between a piece that lasts one generation and one that becomes a family heirloom.

Common Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
Can I clean my jewelry at home between visits?
Yes — a gentle soak in warm water with a drop of mild dish soap and a soft toothbrush is safe for most gold and diamond jewelry. However, avoid home ultrasonic cleaners for rings with certain stones (emeralds, opals, pearls, or other soft gems), and avoid harsh chemicals like bleach or acetone. When in doubt, bring it in.
How long does a professional cleaning take?
A professional ultrasonic and steam cleaning typically takes 15–30 minutes. You can often wait while it's done. For deep cleaning combined with an inspection, plan for around 30–45 minutes.
Is the JM Care Plan covered by jewelry insurance?
Many jewelry insurance policies require documented regular inspections to maintain coverage. Our professional inspection records can serve as documentation for insurance purposes. Check your policy details and ask us about providing written inspection reports.
What stones should not be ultrasonically cleaned?
Ultrasonic cleaning can damage certain soft or porous stones: emeralds (often oiled), opals, pearls, turquoise, coral, tanzanite, and some treated stones. We always assess the stones in your piece before choosing the appropriate cleaning method to ensure nothing is damaged.
How do I know if I need the care plan or a repair?
If your jewelry is in good condition and just needs maintenance — bring it in for a care visit. If something looks wrong, feels wrong, or you suspect damage, bring it in for a free assessment and we'll recommend the right service. There's no charge to look at your piece.
